
Product Requirements Document (PRD): Real Power Tech Platform
Version: 1.0
Date: October 19, 2025
Introduction: This document provides the detailed functional and design specifications for the Real Power Tech WiFi Monetization Platform. It serves as the primary guide for the design, development, and testing of the application, translating the project's vision into actionable requirements.

Part 1: User Personas
Purpose: To create a deep, shared understanding of our key users, their goals, motivations, and critical pain points. Every feature we build must directly address the needs of these personas.
1.1 The Operator: "David, The Tech Entrepreneur"
Bio: David is the founder of Real Power Tech Ltd. He is tech-savvy, business-minded, and focused on creating scalable, automated systems that generate recurring revenue with low operational overhead. He understands the technology but needs a platform that allows him to manage the business, not just the network.
Goals:
To have a centralized "command center" to monitor the financial performance of his entire Wi-Fi network in real-time.
To easily and quickly onboard new partners and deploy new hotspot locations to expand his business footprint.
To automate the revenue-sharing calculation and generate transparent, trustworthy reports that make partner payouts simple and error-free.
To spend his time growing the business (finding new partners) rather than manually managing individual routers or payments.
Frustrations (Pain Points):
Manual Accounting is a Nightmare: "If I have 50 locations, I can't spend days manually cross-referencing transaction logs with partner agreements. It's inefficient, prone to errors, and kills my ability to scale."
Lack of a Holistic View: "Without a central dashboard, I'm flying blind. I don't know which locations are performing well, what my total revenue is, or how many people are using the service right now."
Onboarding Complexity: "Setting up a new location and linking it to the right partner needs to be a simple, repeatable process. If it's a complex technical task every time, I can't grow quickly."
How the Platform Helps David: The platform is his complete business management tool. The Admin Dashboard gives him the real-time, holistic view he needs. The Partner and Location Management features make expansion a simple, form-based process. Most importantly, the automated Financial Reports feature solves his biggest pain point by eliminating manual accounting, allowing him to scale his partnerships with confidence.
1.2 The Partner: "Maria, The Cafe Owner"
Bio: Maria owns a popular, busy cafe in a city center. Her customers are constantly asking for Wi-Fi. She's not very technical and is focused on her core business: making great coffee and providing excellent service. She sees Wi-Fi as a necessary amenity but is intrigued by the idea of it generating revenue instead of being a cost.
Goals:
To offer reliable Wi-Fi to her customers without having to manage it herself.
To earn a passive income stream from her existing foot traffic with zero effort.
To have a simple, trustworthy relationship with the Operator (David), where she gets a clear statement and a fair payment at the end of the month.
Frustrations (Pain Points):
Technical Complexity: "I don't know how to configure a router or manage a network. I just want something that works. If it breaks, I need someone else to fix it."
Payment Hassles: "I don't want my staff to have to sell Wi-Fi vouchers or handle cash for internet access. It's a distraction and complicates my bookkeeping."
Lack of Trust in Partnerships: "If I partner with someone, I need to know I'm getting my fair share. I need a clear, transparent report that shows me exactly how much revenue my location generated."
How the Platform Helps Maria: The platform is a "fire-and-forget" solution for her. David's team installs the pre-configured router, and she never has to touch it again. The automated customer journey means her staff is never involved in the transaction. The transparent financial reports generated by the platform provide the trust and clarity she needs in the partnership, making it a simple and valuable addition to her business.
1.3 The Customer: "John, The University Student"
Bio: John is a student who frequently studies at Maria's cafe. He needs reliable internet to work on assignments and browse the web. He's always on his phone or laptop and is accustomed to seamless digital experiences. He's willing to pay a small amount for good, fast internet, but the process has to be quick and easy.
Goals:
To get online quickly and with minimal fuss.
To have clear, upfront pricing and package options.
To use a familiar and secure payment method, like his mobile money account.
Frustrations (Pain Points):
Complicated Sign-Up Forms: "I hate having to create a new account with a username and password just to use Wi-Fi for an hour. I don't want to give my personal information or remember another password."
Awkward Payment Process: "I don't want to have to go to the counter and pay with cash to get a paper voucher with a long code I have to type in. It feels outdated and inconvenient."
Uncertainty and Lack of Clarity: "I just want to see the options, pick one, pay, and get online. If the process is confusing, I'll just use my mobile data."
How the Platform Helps John: The platform provides the exact frictionless experience he expects. There is no account creation. The Captive Portal is clean and simple, showing clear options. The redirection to the official ClickPesa gateway is a familiar, secure process. For John, the entire journey from connecting to the Wi-Fi to being online takes less than a minute, which is exactly what he wants.
Part 2: Key User Flows & Wireframe Descriptions
Purpose: To outline the critical user journeys and describe the primary screens required to facilitate them. This section will serve as the blueprint for the UI/UX design, ensuring a logical and intuitive user experience.
2.1 Flow: Operator Onboards a Partner & Registers a New Hotspot
Goal: For David (our Operator persona) to add a new partner (Maria's Cafe) to the system and then register and assign a new physical router to that partner, making it ready for deployment.
Steps:
David logs into the Operator Admin Dashboard.
He navigates to the "Partner Management" section.
He clicks "Add New Partner," fills in Maria's details and her revenue share percentage, and saves.
He then navigates to the "Device & Location Management" section.
He clicks "Add New Location," fills in the details for the new router (e.g., "Maria's Cafe on Main St," MAC address).
In the "Assign to Partner" dropdown, he selects Maria's newly created profile.
He saves the location. The router is now logically linked to Maria's partnership and is ready to be physically installed at her cafe.
Wireframe Descriptions:
2.1.1 Operator Admin Dashboard (Main Hub):
Layout: A clean, professional dashboard with clear navigation and key metrics.
Components:
Header: "Welcome, David."
At-a-Glance Stat Cards: Large, clear cards for "Total Revenue," "Total Users Connected," "Active Locations," and "Active Partners."
Recent Transactions Feed: A live-updating list of the last few transactions from across the network.
Main Navigation (Side Bar): Secure, persistent links to "Dashboard," "Partner Management," "Device & Location Management," "Service Packages," and "Financial Reports."
2.1.2 Partner Management Screen:
Layout: A standard list-management page with controls at the top and a data table below.
Components:
Header: "Partner Management."
Primary Button (Top-Right): "Add New Partner."
Partner List Table: A searchable table with columns for Partner Name, Assigned Locations (count), and Revenue Share %. Each row has "Edit" and "Remove" actions.
2.1.3 Add/Edit Partner Modal:
Layout: A simple, focused form that appears in a modal dialog.
Components:
Header: "Add New Partner."
Input Fields: First Name, Last Name, Email.
Revenue Share % Input: A number input field, clearly labeled, for the agreed-upon percentage.
Primary Button: "Save Partner."
2.1.4 Device & Location Management Screen:
Layout: A standard list-management page, similar to the Partner screen.
Components:
Header: "Device & Location Management."
Primary Button (Top-Right): "Add New Location."
Location List Table: A searchable table with columns for Location Name, Assigned Partner, Router Identifier (MAC Address), and Status (Active/Inactive). Each row has "Edit" and "Deactivate" actions.
2.1.5 Add/Edit Location Modal:
Layout: A simple, focused form in a modal dialog.
Components:
Header: "Register New Hotspot Location."
Input Fields: Location Name, Router Model, Router Identifier.
The Critical Link: A required dropdown menu labeled "Assign to Partner," populated with the list of all existing partners. This is a non-skippable step.
Primary Button: "Save Location."
2.2 Flow: The Automated Customer Journey
Goal: For John (our Customer persona) to connect to the Wi-Fi at Maria's Cafe, purchase a one-hour pass, and get online seamlessly and securely.
Steps:
John opens his laptop and connects to the "Real Power Tech - Pay & Surf" Wi-Fi network.
His browser is automatically opened and redirected to the platform's Captive Portal.
He sees the available packages and selects the "1-Hour Access" option.
He is automatically redirected to the secure ClickPesa payment page.
He completes the payment with his mobile money account.
Upon successful payment, he is redirected back to a simple "Success!" page from the platform.
The system has already authorized his device in the background. He can now open a new tab and browse the internet freely.
After one hour, his session automatically expires, and he is disconnected.
Wireframe Descriptions:
2.2.1 The Captive Portal Screen:
Layout: A clean, mobile-first, single-column layout designed for clarity and speed.
Components:
Header: Prominently features the REAL POWERTECH LTD logo.
Welcome Text: "Welcome to [Location Name]!" (The location name can be dynamically inserted if passed by the router). "Please select a package to get online."
Service Package List: A series of large, easily tappable cards. Each card clearly displays:
Package Name: (e.g., "1-Hour Access")
Price: (e.g., "1,000 Tsh")
No other clutter: No ads, no complicated text, no sign-up forms. The sole focus is on selecting a product.
2.2.2 The "Success & Connected" Screen:
Layout: A very simple, static confirmation page.
Components:
Icon: A large checkmark icon.
Header: "You are connected!"
Subtext: "Your session is valid for [Duration]. You can now close this page and browse the internet."
2.3 Flow: Operator Generates a Partner Payout Report
Goal: At the end of the month, David needs to generate a report to accurately pay Maria her share of the revenue generated by her cafe's hotspot.
Steps:
David logs into the Operator Admin Dashboard.
He navigates to the "Financial Reports" section.
He uses the filter controls to select "Maria's Cafe" (or Maria as a partner) from the dropdown list.
He selects the date range for the previous month.
He clicks "Generate Report."
The system instantly displays a clear, detailed report on the screen, which he can use to make the payout.
Wireframe Descriptions:
2.3.1 Financial Reports Screen:
Layout: A simple control section at the top with the report output displayed below.
Components:
Header: "Financial Reports."
Filter Controls:
Partner Dropdown: A dropdown to select a specific partner.
Date Range Picker: A calendar-based tool to select a start and end date.
Primary Button: "Generate Report."
2.3.2 The Generated Report View:
Layout: A clean, printable report format.
Components:
Report Header: "Payout Report for: [Partner Name]," "Period: [Start Date] - [End Date]."
Summary Metrics: A clear, boxed-off section at the top showing the most important numbers:
Total Revenue Generated: (e.g., 150,000 Tsh)
Partner's Share (40%): (e.g., 60,000 Tsh)
Partner Payout Amount: (e.g., 60,000 Tsh)
Operator's Share (60%): (e.g., 90,000 Tsh)
Detailed Transaction List: A full, paginated table providing transparency.
Columns: Transaction ID, Date/Time, Location Name, Package Purchased, Amount.
Footer: Shows the sum of the "Amount" column, which must match the "Total Revenue Generated" in the summary.

Part 3: Detailed Functional Requirements (User Stories)
Purpose: To break down the high-level project goals into specific, testable user stories. This provides a clear checklist for the development team to build from and for stakeholders to validate.
3.1 Operator Features
Authentication & Dashboard
3.1.1: As the Operator (David), I want a secure login page with email and password fields so that I can access my Admin Dashboard.
3.1.2: As the Operator, after logging in, I want to land on a dashboard that shows me key, real-time metrics (Total Revenue, Total Users Connected, Active Locations, Active Partners) so I can get an immediate snapshot of my business's health.
3.1.3: As the Operator, I want to see a live feed of the last 5-10 transactions on my dashboard so I can see recent activity across my network.
Partner Management
3.1.4: As the Operator, I want to be able to create a new Partner by providing their name, email, and a specific Revenue Share Percentage, so I can officially add them to my business network.
3.1.5: As the Operator, I want to view a list of all my partners, showing their name, the number of locations they manage, and their revenue share percentage, so I can easily manage my business relationships.
3.1.6: As the Operator, I want to be able to edit an existing partner's details (e.g., update their name or adjust their revenue share percentage) to keep my records accurate.
3.1.7: As the Operator, I want to be able to remove a partner from the system, but only if they have no active locations assigned to them, to ensure data integrity and prevent orphaned financial records.
Device & Location Management
3.1.8: As the Operator, I want to register a new Hotspot Location by providing a descriptive name, the router model, and its unique identifier (MAC address).
3.1.9: As the Operator, when registering a new location, I must assign it to an existing partner from a dropdown list, so that all future revenue from this device is correctly attributed.
3.1.10: As the Operator, I want to view a list of all my registered locations, showing which partner they are assigned to and their status (Active/Inactive), so I have a complete inventory of my hardware assets.
3.1.11: As the Operator, I want to be able to edit a location's details, such as its name or re-assigning it to a different partner.
3.1.12: As the Operator, I want to be able to deactivate a location to temporarily take it offline without deleting its historical data.
Service Package Management
3.1.13: As the Operator, I want a settings page where I can create a new Service Package by defining its name (e.g., "Weekly Pass"), its price (in Tsh), and its duration (in minutes).
3.1.14: As the Operator, I want all Service Packages to be managed globally, so that the offerings and prices are consistent across all locations in my network.
3.1.15: As the Operator, I want to be able to edit an existing package's price or duration, or deactivate it so it no longer appears as an option for new customers.

Financial Reports
3.1.16: As the Operator, I want a reporting tool where I can select any of my partners and a specific date range.
3.1.17: As the Operator, after selecting a partner and date range, I want to click a "Generate Report" button and see a clear summary showing the Total Revenue generated by that partner's locations, their Revenue Share Percentage, and the final calculated Payout Amount.
3.1.18: As the Operator, I want the generated report to include a detailed, itemized list of every single transaction that was included in the calculation, so I can provide full transparency to my partner.
3.2 Customer Features
Connection & Portal Experience
3.2.1: As a Customer (John), when I connect to the public Wi-Fi at a partner venue, I want my web browser to be automatically redirected to a Captive Portal page.
3.2.2: As a Customer, I want the Captive Portal to clearly display a list of available internet packages with their duration and price so I can easily choose the one I want.
3.2.3: As a Customer, I want the purchasing process to be "accountless," meaning I do not have to create a username, password, or provide any personal information to buy access.
Payment & Access
3.2.4: As a Customer, after selecting a package, I want to be redirected to the official and secure ClickPesa payment gateway to complete my transaction using my mobile money account.
3.2.5: As a Customer, once my payment is successful, I want to receive immediate, uninterrupted internet access without any further steps.
3.2.6: As a Customer, I understand and expect that my internet session will automatically terminate once the duration I purchased has expired.
3.3 System & Backend Requirements
3.3.1: The system must have a dedicated webhook endpoint to securely receive payment confirmation notifications from ClickPesa.
3.3.2: Upon receiving a successful payment webhook, the system must instantly update the corresponding transaction's status from "Pending" to "Completed."
3.3.3: Immediately after a transaction is marked as "Completed," the system must communicate with the RADIUS server to authorize the customer's device (identified by its MAC address) for the specific duration of the purchased package.
3.3.4: The system's communication between the on-site routers and the central RADIUS server must be secured using a shared secret key.

Part 4: Technical Specifications & Platform Requirements
Purpose: To define the technologies, architecture, and standards that will govern the development of the platform. This ensures the system is not only functional but also secure, performant, and reliable.
4.1 Core Technology Stack
Frontend Framework: Next.js with TypeScript.
UI Components: A modern component library such as Shadcn UI, built upon Tailwind CSS for rapid and consistent styling.
Backend Framework: Next js API routes.
Database: MongoDB with mongoose.
RADIUS Server: A dedicated FreeRADIUS server instance.
4.2 Third-Party Service Integrations
Payment Gateway: ClickPesa is the exclusive payment gateway for all mobile money and bank transactions.
Hardware: The system must be compatible with MikroTik routers (specifically models like the hAP ax² and hAP ax³ that support captive portal and RADIUS authentication).
4.3 Deployment & Hosting Infrastructure
Frontend & Backend (Web App): Vercel for the Next.js application, providing optimal performance, CI/CD, and scalability.
RADIUS Server: A reliable, "always-on" infrastructure-as-a-service provider like Fly.io or a dedicated virtual private server (VPS), which is well-suited for long-running stateful services.
Database: A managed database service MongoDB Atlas to handle backups, scaling, and security.
4.4 Non-Functional Requirements
Security:
The Operator Admin Dashboard must be protected by a secure authentication system with hashed passwords.
All web traffic (to the Captive Portal and Admin Dashboard) must be encrypted via HTTPS.
The platform must not store any sensitive customer payment information (e.g., mobile money numbers). All payment processing is delegated to the PCI-compliant ClickPesa gateway.
Communication between the backend and the RADIUS server, and between the RADIUS server and the routers, must be secured with a strong shared secret.
Performance:
The Captive Portal must load in under 2 seconds on a standard 3G connection to prevent customer drop-off.
The API response time for the webhook confirmation must be under 500ms to ensure the customer gets access as quickly as possible after payment.
The Operator Admin Dashboard pages should load in under 3 seconds.
Reliability:
The RADIUS server and the webhook endpoint must maintain a minimum uptime of 99.9%, as downtime directly translates to lost revenue.
The production database must have automated daily backups enabled to ensure data integrity and disaster recovery.
Scalability:
The architecture must be able to handle hundreds of concurrent customer connection requests and payment transactions without performance degradation.

Part 5: Out-of-Scope (What We Are Not Building for MVP)
Purpose: To clearly define the boundaries of this project. Explicitly stating what is not included is crucial for managing stakeholder expectations and ensuring the development team remains focused on delivering the core value proposition on time.
Partner-Facing Dashboard: Partners (e.g., Maria, the cafe owner) will not have a login or a self-service portal to view their own performance. All financial reporting will be generated and provided to them by the Operator. This simplifies the MVP by removing an entire user role and its associated permissions and interfaces.
Automated Payouts: The platform's role is to provide definitive and transparent accounting to inform payouts. It will not integrate with banking or mobile money APIs to automatically transfer the partner's share to their account. The Operator will perform this action manually based on the generated reports.
Customer Accounts & Session History: Customers are transient. The platform will not have any features for customers to create accounts, view their purchase history, or manage a profile. A customer's identity is limited to their device's MAC address for a single session.
Voucher or Code-Based Access: The platform will only support direct, on-portal payments. There will be no system for generating, printing, or redeeming prepaid access codes or vouchers.
On-Portal Advertising: The Captive Portal is a point of sale, not an advertising space. There will be no functionality to serve third-party ads on the portal.
Advanced Data Analytics & Visualization: The Operator's dashboard and reports will provide clear, raw numbers and itemized lists. The MVP will not include complex graphs, charts, trend analysis, or predictive analytics.
Multiple Payment Gateways: Integration with Stripe or other international payment gateways is not in scope. ClickPesa is the sole gateway for the MVP.
